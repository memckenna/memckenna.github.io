Recipe #1: Overnight Oats

  • Time: 8hrs
  • Serves: 1 person
Ingredients Quantity
Old-Fashioned Oats 1/2 Cup
Almond Milk 1/2 Cup
Greek Yogurt 1/4 Cup
Maple Syrup 1-2 TSP
Chia Seeds 1 TSP
Vanilla Extract 1-2 TSP

Step 1: Add oats, milk, yogurt, maple syrup, chia seeds, vanilla and sea salt into a jar or storage container with a lid. Stir ingredients together. Place in the fridge overnight. If youâ€™re in a rush you can shorten the soak time to 2-4 hours.

Step 2: The next morning (or when ready to eat), remove lid and give oats a big stir. If they seem too thick you can add a little more milk to loosen the mixture. Top with your favorite toppings and enjoy. You can eat the oats straight from the jar or pour into a bowl for serving.

Step 3: Overnight oats should last up to 5 days in the fridge in a sealed airtight container.

Recipe #2: Quinoa Salad

  • Time: 25 minutes
  • Serves: 4 people
Ingredients Quantity
Uncooked Quinoa 1/2 Cup
Water 1 Cup
Chopped Baby Spinach 1/2 Cup
Chopped Cherry Tomatos 1/2 Cup
Lemon Juice 2 TBS
Olive Oil 2 TBS
Sea Salt 1/2 TSP
Ground Pepper 1/4 TSP
Ripe Avocado 2
Crumbled Feta Cheese 1/3 Cup

Step 1: Cook Quinoa: Rinse and drain quinoa. Add quinoa and water to a saucepan and bring the water to a boil. Reduce to a simmer, cover and cook for 15 minutes. Fluff with a fork and let cool for 5-10 minutes.

Step 2: Toss and Whisk: In a medium bowl, stir together cooked quinoa, chopped spinach, tomatoes, red onion and feta. In a small bowl whisk together lemon juice, olive oil, salt and pepper. Add dressing and avocado: Gently stir in dressing and avocado chunks.

Step 3: Chill and enjoy: Serve immediately or let the salad chill in the fridge a couple hours before serving. If chilling, you can add the avocado right before serving to prevent browning.

Recipe #3: Roasted Broccoli

  • Time: 40 minutes
  • Serves: 2 people
Ingredients Quantity
Broccoli 2 Crowns
Olive Oil 1 TBS
Sea Salt 1 TSP
Ground Pepper 1/2 TSP
Chopped Garlic 1 TBS
Cayenne Pepper 1/2 TSP

Step 1: Prep Broccoli: Cut the broccoli florets from the thick stalks, leaving an inch or two of stalk attached to the florets. Save the stalks for another use.

Step 2: Add oil and Seasoning: Coat broccoli florets with the oil in a large bowl or right on the baking sheet. You can use your hands to make sure the broccoli is well coated. Sprinkle on the salt, pepper, garlic and cayenne pepper (if using) and toss to coat.

Step 3: Roast and Serve Immediately: Put the broccoli on a rimmed baking sheet and place in the oven for about 25-30 minutes or until broccoli is cooked and the tops of the broccoli are a little brown. Serve immediately.
